    App Development

    Contributed to the release of two music-making apps: Zya, and Ditty.

    Zya

    Zya was an automated music composition app that allowed anyone to create new music from scratch, or mix and match with stems from famous artists. The system handled vocal transcription, key signature detection, pitch-tuning and correction, automatic harmonization, dynamic transposition and arrangement of MIDI patterns, cloud-based rendering and streaming, and automatic mixing and mastering. I was personally involved or driving every one of those research projects. Here is a demo of how Zya worked.

    Ditty

    Ditty was our 2nd music app, in a push to streamline the creation process even more. For Ditty, I was the Director of Research and Development, and was solely responsible for the musical engine which took a user's text, broke down the grammatical and phrase structure, segmented melodic phrases of the chosen song, aligned the user's text to the melody using optional melismas or melodic embellishments, synthesized the vocals and streamed it down to the device. You can still see what people are currently creating with Ditty through the #ditty search on Twitter. Ditty won the Best Music App award for 2015 through the Appy Awards, and currently has over 5 million users.
